<p><p>Automotive glow plugs are categorized into hot and cold glow plugs based on their operating temperatures. Hot glow plugs are used in diesel engines where high temperatures are required for combustion, while cold glow plugs are used in gasoline engines where lower temperatures are sufficient. Additionally, glow plugs are classified as metal or ceramic based on the materials used in their construction. Metal glow plugs are more durable and provide faster heating, while ceramic glow plugs offer better insulation and are more resistant to corrosion.</p></p>
