<p><p>Dual-fuel engines are classified into two main types: Four-stroke dual-fuel engines and Two-stroke dual-fuel engines. Four-stroke dual-fuel engines operate on a four-stroke combustion cycle, combining diesel with natural gas or other alternative fuels. On the other hand, Two-stroke dual-fuel engines use a two-stroke combustion cycle and also mix diesel with natural gas to generate power. Both types offer improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions compared to traditional diesel engines, making them increasingly popular in the market.</p></p>
