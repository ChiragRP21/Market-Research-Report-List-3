<p><p>3D Arterial Spin Labeling (3D ASL) is a non-invasive MRI technique used to measure cerebral blood flow. This technique is employed in various market types such as continuous arterial spin labeling (CASL) and pulsed arterial spin labeling (PASL). CASL involves continuous labeling and ASL measurements throughout the blood circulation, while PASL uses pulsed labeling and measures blood flow after the labeled blood reaches the target tissue. Both CASL and PASL have applications in clinical research, diagnosis of neurological diseases, and monitoring treatment efficacy.</p></p>
