<p><p>Ceramic bearings are made from high-quality ceramic material, typically silicon nitride or zirconia, which offers excellent wear resistance, corrosion resistance, and high temperature capabilities. These bearings are commonly used in applications where traditional steel bearings may fail. On the other hand, carbon graphite bearings are known for their self-lubricating properties, high thermal conductivity, and low coefficient of friction. They are often used in high-temperature, high-speed applications where traditional lubricants may not be suitable.</p></p>
