<p><p> Biobased surfactants are categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, nonionic, and amphoteric. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge and are commonly used in cleaning products. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are known for their conditioning properties in personal care products. Nonionic surfactants are uncharged and are gentle on the skin, making them popular in skincare products. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charges, providing versatility in various applications. Each type plays a unique role in the biobased surfactants market.</p></p>
