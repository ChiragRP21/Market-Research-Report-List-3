<p><p>Data center colocation is a service that allows businesses to rent space within a third-party facility to house their servers and other computing equipment. Retail colocation refers to smaller-scale arrangements where businesses rent smaller portions of space, such as a rack or a few cabinets, catering to their specific requirements. Wholesale colocation, on the other hand, involves larger-scale commitments, where businesses lease significant portions of the data center, often an entire floor or building, providing them with greater flexibility and scalability options. Both types provide businesses with reliable infrastructure, power, cooling, and connectivity services.</p></p>
