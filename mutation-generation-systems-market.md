<p><p>Mutation generation systems are tools used for introducing specific mutations into genes for research purposes. Different types of mutations can be generated using these systems, including missense mutations (a single nucleotide change leading to a different amino acid), nonsense mutations (a single nucleotide change leading to a premature stop codon), insertions (addition of nucleotides), deletions (removal of nucleotides), duplications (repeated sequences of nucleotides), frame shift mutations (insertion or deletion of nucleotides shifting the reading frame), and repeat expansions (increase in the number of repeated sequences).</p></p>
