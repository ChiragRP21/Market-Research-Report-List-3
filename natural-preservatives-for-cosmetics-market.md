<p><p>Natural preservatives for cosmetics can be categorized into four main types: lipids, acids, alcohols, and others. Lipid-based preservatives such as oils and butters help to maintain the integrity of the product by creating a barrier against bacterial growth. Acids like citric acid and sorbic acid work by lowering the pH of the product, creating an environment that is unfavorable for microbes. Alcohols such as ethanol and benzyl alcohol disrupt the cell membranes of bacteria and fungi. Other natural preservatives include plant extracts, antioxidants, and essential oils. These different types of preservatives work together to extend the shelf life of cosmetics while maintaining their safety and efficacy.</p></p>
