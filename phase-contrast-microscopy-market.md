<p><ul><li>Traditional Phase-Contrast Microscopy</li><li>Quantitative Phase-Contrast Microscopy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phase contrast microscopy is a technique used to enhance the contrast of transparent specimens without the need for staining. Traditional phase-contrast microscopy uses optical mechanisms to create contrast in the image. Quantitative phase-contrast microscopy, on the other hand, measures the phase shifts of light passing through the specimen to generate quantitative data about the sample. Both types of phase contrast microscopy are used in various fields such as biology, medicine, and materials science for detailed imaging of transparent samples.</p></p>
