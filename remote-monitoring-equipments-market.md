<p><p>External remote monitoring equipment refers to devices that can continuously monitor a patient's health status from outside the body, such as wearable devices or sensors. These devices are typically worn by the patient and transmit data to healthcare providers for analysis. On the other hand, implantable remote monitoring equipment is embedded inside the patient's body to monitor specific health conditions, such as pacemakers or defibrillators. Both types of equipment play a crucial role in enhancing patient care and improving treatment outcomes.</p></p>
