<p><p>RF/Microwave inductors are key components in electronics for storing and releasing energy in the form of magnetic fields. There are three main types of RF/Microwave inductors in the market: wire wound type, film type, and multilayer type. Wire wound inductors are wound around a core, film inductors are made from thin film layers, and multilayer inductors consist of multiple layers of conductor and insulator materials. Each type has its own advantages and applications based on factors such as frequency range, size, and performance requirements.</p></p>
