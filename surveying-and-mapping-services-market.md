<p><p>The surveying and mapping services market can be classified into various types. Hydrographic surveying involves measuring and mapping underwater environments, primarily for maritime purposes. Cadastral surveying focuses on establishing and mapping land boundaries, often for legal and property registration purposes. Topographic surveying entails mapping the natural and man-made features of a land area, such as elevation and terrain. Lastly, the "Others" category includes specialized surveying services like geodetic surveying for precise positioning and engineering surveying for construction and infrastructure projects. These market types differentiate the various applications and requirements within the broader surveying and mapping services sector.</p></p>
