<p><p>Food-grade phosphates, including Sodium Tripolyphosphate (STPP), Sodium Hexametaphosphate (SHMP), Sodium Acid Pyrophosphate (SAPP), and Tetrasodium Pyrophosphate (TSPP), are commonly used in the food industry as functional ingredients and preservatives. STPP is widely used as a water-retention agent and emulsifier in processed meats and seafood. SHMP is used as a sequestrant in meat processing to improve texture and prevent spoilage. SAPP is a leavening agent in baked goods, while TSPP is used as a buffering agent in dairy products and canned vegetables.</p></p>
